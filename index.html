80 YEARS OF SEARCH
interactive research of family memory
A story about fear passed down through generations, and the emptiness left behind by the Soviet repressive system.

ACADEMIC CONTEXT: This is an academic visual game developed as part of the research framework: "The Migration of Silence and the Inherited Myths of Loyalty: Family Memory and Bureaucratic Folklore in Post-Soviet Space". Created for 'Folklore on the Move' The Folklore Society's Annual Conference 2026 (In Person: 5-7 June at King's College, University of Aberdeen; Online: 10 June 2026).

ABOUT: This project is entirely based on personal experience, reflections, and deep research into the actual archive case files regarding the repression of my ancestor. The game consists of 4 logical mini-games.

HOW TO PLAY: Answer as honestly as you would in real life. In most cases, there are no generic right or wrong answers, but keep in mind that every choice carries weight and shapes the final outcome.

OBJECTIVE: To provide an opportunity to gain a new perspective and experience through emotional engagement.

FEEDBACK: If you would like to share your thoughts, ideas, or suggestions, feel free to reach out to me directly at n.neborachko@gmail.com.
